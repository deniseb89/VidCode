1. What are we writing? JavaScript!

JavaScript?

Javascript is a programming language. Since computers don't speak human languages like English or Spanish, we use programming languages to talk to them.

All your favorite apps are made by talking to computers with programming languages.

2. How is it changing my video?

Your code is your input, and your video is the output created once the computer translates your code.

When you change the words and numbers in your code (your input), you'll see your video (your output) change as well.

Think about it like baking. Depending on what ingredients you put into your cake (your output) will be different.

3. The pieces of our code

Some of the pieces of JavaScript include values, variables, objects and functions.

Have you changed the numbers and words around in your code? Then you've already worked with some of these pieces!

First, let's talk about values.

4. Let's talk about values

The numbers in your code, such as

effects.noise.amount = 7;

are a type of value called a number (but you already knew that!)

4a. Types of Values

Some types of values are:

Numbers: 1, 12, 178

Strings (text in quotes): "hello", "12 is a cool number"

Booleans (whether something is true or false): videoPlaying = true;

4b. Booleans

Booleans set variables to true or false.

5. Another value: objects!

Let's move on to a fourth type of value, objects!

Objects in JavaScript hold your data.

Think about a picnic basket, that holds the lots of different things that we need for a picnic. Our one basket can hold foods, such as salads and cheese, but it can also hold our picnic blanket and plates.

In your code, effects and movie are both objects.

5a. Your movie!

The word "movie" is an object that uses the canvas element in HTML5 to play your video.

Remember, an object is just a word that contains data under the hood.

With code, and computers, there are many things happening "behind the scenes" which we don't see.

5b. So many effects!

The word effects is an object that keeps track of all of the effects you can apply to your video, such as blur and vignette.

6. Functions

A function is a an instruction. You can think of it like a verb.

Imagine if you were training your cat. If you gave your cat an obstacle course, and told her to complete it, your cat would probably be pretty confused.

But if instead, you broke it up into smaller instructions, and told her

'go down this slide'

it would be much less confusing.

Functions are kind of like that! Lots of smaller instructions to make coding easier.

play() is an example of a function.

6a. play() is a function

When you type 'play()' or 'pause()' after the word movie, you are sending a function (a verb!) directly to the movie object.

"interval" is also a method

It tells the movie object how many times a second to be processed.

The number "1000" is in milliseconds. So it is processing your video 1,000 times a second! That's a lot of work!
